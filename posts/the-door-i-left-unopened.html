The Door I Left Unopened

January 2025 · 5 min read

As a child, I wanted to be everything at once. An astronaut. A designer. A pastry chef. Each dream felt real, urgent, possible. The world was wide and I had all the time in it.

Somewhere along the way, I chose one door and quietly closed the others. Not dramatically, not with any sense of loss at the time. Just naturally, the way rivers find their course and eventually forget the other paths they could have taken.

This piece is about wondering not regretting who I might have been if I had chosen differently. What would my hands know if I'd become a baker? What skies would I have seen as an astronaut? What colors would I dream in as a designer?

Maybe the doors we don't open still shape us, just in quieter ways. They live in the books we're drawn to, the hobbies we pick up in spare moments, the conversations that make our eyes light up unexpectedly.

I think there's something beautiful in carrying all these unlived lives with us. They're not failures or losses. They're possibilities that remind us we are always more than the single path we walk.
